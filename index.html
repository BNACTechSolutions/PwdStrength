Some Do's and Don'ts for a Strong Password

Do's:

  • Use at least 12 characters. The longer, the better.
  • Include a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols.
  • Use a different password for each account to limit damage in case of a breach.
  • Use Multi-Factor Authentication. Pair your password with an additional layer of security.

Don'ts:

  • Don't use easily guessable passwords like.
  • Avoid using your name, birthday, phone number, or any public information.
  • Don't rely on sequence like abcd, 1234, or keyboard patterns like qwerty.
  • Don't reuse the same password across multiple sites.
  • Don't write down your passwords on sticky notes or notebooks.
  • Never share your password, even with trusted people.
